Current Work

My research explores how fungal parasites of diatoms influence gene expression, metabolism, and nutrient flow in freshwater ecosystems. I combine transcriptomics, cell counts, SIMS (for single-cell nutrient uptake), and chemical analyses to understand how infections reshape the host and its microbial community.

Using a new Asterionella genome and 30 co-occurring bacterial MAGs, I investigate how infection stages affect transcription in the host and its microbiome. Late-stage infections show host upregulation in fatty acid and photosynthesis pathways, alongside increased bacterial expression of nutrient degradation and vitamin transport genes. These patterns suggest fungi remodel host metabolism in ways that facilitate bacterial access to resources.
